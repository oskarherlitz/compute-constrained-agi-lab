# Thesis v0: Useful Intelligence per Watt-Dollar

## Core question

How do we maximize useful intelligence under real-world constraints such as compute, power, capital, latency, trust, and deployment?

## Claim

The path to useful AGI is a full-stack race across model architecture, compute, power, inference economics, deployment reliability, data center siting, materials, energy, water, and organizational execution.

A builder who wants to industrialize intelligence must understand the whole supply chain.

## What this project is

Compute-Constrained AGI Lab is a public artifact that makes those constraints concrete:

1. **Compute economics simulator** — model training cost, inference cost, power draw, and cost per successful task under explicit assumptions.
2. **AI infrastructure atlas** — map where compute can physically scale (power, price, water, interconnection).
3. **Agent reliability benchmark** — measure whether AI workflows complete useful tasks reliably enough to deploy.
4. **Founder memo** — synthesize what the bottlenecks to industrializing AGI actually are.

## North-star metric

**Useful intelligence per watt-dollar.**

Intelligence that cannot be verified, trusted, or economically deployed is not yet a force multiplier. Cost per successful task is the deployment-side version of the same idea.

## Open questions

- What is the real binding constraint today: compute, power, capital, reliability, or latency?
- How much does test-time compute change inference economics at scale?
- Where can compute physically scale, and what stops it?
- What success rate does an agent workflow need before unit economics work?
